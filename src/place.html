    Maimun Palace

    Maimoon Palace or Maimun Palace is a royal palace of the Sultanate of Deli and a well-known landmark in Medan, the capital city of Northern Sumatra, Indonesia. Today, it serves as a museum.

    Built by Sultan Ma'mun Al Rashid Perkasa Alamyah in years 1887–1891, the palace was designed by the Dutch architect Theodoor van Erp and covers 2,772 m² with a total of 30 rooms. The Palace has become a popular tourist destination in the city, not solely because of its historical heritage status, but also because of its unique interior design of the palace, combining elements of Malay cultural heritage, Islamic and Indian architecture, with Spanish and Italian furniture and fittings.

    The color that dominates is yellow because yellow is considered the color of the greatness of the Deli Kingdom in North Sumatra as well as symbolizing the color of Malay. There are three parts in Maimun Palace, namely the main part and two more on the left and right wings. In the main part, there is a large room that was once used for traditional activities. Now, it is used to view the collection of royal relics.

    What's more interesting there is the existence of a small building with a thatched roof on the right side of the palace. There is a Meriam Puntung which is also called Meriam Buntung with the legend of the incarnation of a beautiful princess. Meriam Puntung is the incarnation of Putri Hijau's sister from the Old Deli Kingdom named Mambang Khayali who was beautiful.

    Tjong A Fie Mansion

    Tjong A Fie migrated to Medan to try his luck and met his older brother Tjong Yong Hian, who had already come to Medan and lived for 5 years. His brother at that time had become the leader of the Chinese tribe. Tjong A Fie was known to be very sociable and later learned the Malay language so that he sometimes became an intermediary between the Chinese and the Malays.

    Tjong A Fie is a role model for many people, so he often mediates when there is a dispute between the Chinese and other tribes. With his leadership spirit, Tjong A fie was often asked to solve problems that occurred between workers on the plantation, then he was appointed a Chinese lieutenant for his success.

    Tjong A Fie died on February 4, 1921, due to suffering from a brain hemorrhage. The entire community of Medan mourned, thousands of mourners came from various cities. Tjong A Fie's funeral procession took place magnificently in accordance with his tradition and position.

    Tjong A Fie willed part of his wealth in Sumatra and outside Sumatra to the Toen Moek Tong Foundation which was founded in Medan and Sungkow at the time of his death. Tjong also advised that the foundation should help those who are unable to work properly because of their disabilities and help victims of natural disasters regardless of nationality or ethnicity.

    Tip Top Restaurant

    Tip Top was established before Indonesia's independence. Since its establishment in 1934 until now, Tip Top has maintained its way of providing food, cakes, and bread.

    This old restaurant provides a variety of menus ranging from western, Indonesian, Chinese food, bakery, to a cake shop. You must try at least one of Tip-Top's four mainstay menus, namely beef tongue steak, special Tip Top fried rice, Moca Moca Tart Cake or Moorkop Ice, ice cream with Dutch Moorkop cake.

    In addition to offering the atmosphere of an old restaurant, the old building of this restaurant is very well maintained. Tip Top even maintains the use of wood-fired stone fire stoves and Dutch-era machines to maintain its quality.
